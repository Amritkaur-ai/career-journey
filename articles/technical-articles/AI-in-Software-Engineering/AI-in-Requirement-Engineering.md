# AI in Requirement Engineering

## Requirement Engineering

Requirement Engineering is a process of identifying, analyzing, documenting and managing stakeholder requirements to build a successful software system. It ensures that final product aligns with user expectations, operates within technical limitations, and avoids costly rework during development.
Key stages in Requirement Engineering are:

- Requirement Elicitation
- Analysis and Tracebility
- Specification
- Validation and Conflict Resolution
- Management and Prototyping

## AI in Requirement Engineering

AI is transforming requirement engineering by automating documentation, identifying hidden stakeholder needs, and detecting inconsistencies early. Jama Software and Modern Requirements are tools which reduce upto 70% of the manual documentation time and a major drop in review downgrade.

## AI in Requirements Elicitation

This stage involves gathering of raw data through interviews, surveys, and observations. AI can help to perform different types of market analysis in a faster and more through manner. AI assists by generating targeted interview questions, creating surveys, and summarizing user stories.
Tools used:

- IBM's Watson Assistant,
- Google's Dialogflow
- WireGen from Figma
- Freehand AI from Invision

## AI in Analysis and Tracebility

It invloves refinig scattered ideas, resolving conflicting stakeholder requests, and setting priorities. Machine Learning algorithms and natural language processing trace requirement links, group similar feedback themes, and assess the impact of changes accross project matrices.
Tools used:

- Copilot4DevOps
- ChatGPT
- Siemens' Teamcenter

## AI in Specification

It is the documenting of decisions into a software requirements specification (SRS) document to create a blueprint for the developer team. AI can help in capturing higher level specifications from non-technical stakeholders and easily convert it into structured text.
Tools used:

- Bit.ai
- WriteMyPRD
- Writedoc
- Claude

## AI in Validation and Conflict Resolution

It invloves checking that the documented requirements are correct, unambiguous, testable, and truely meet custom needs. AI proactively flags contradictory constraints, identifies missing gaps, and extracts complsince rules against industry regulations.
Tools used:

- SodaGPT
- Numerous.ai
- Great Expectation

## AI in Management and Prototyping

It involves tracking scope and controlling changes to the requirements as the product evolves. Machine learning helps maintain the single source of truth across massive shifts in complex ecosystems.
Tools used:

- James Connect
- Snowflake AI
- ClickUp Brain
- Motion

## Benefits

- Reduce manual documentation effort
- Improves requirement quality and consistency
- Detects ambiguities and conflicts early
- Saves time during requirement analysis
- Improves collaboration among stakeholders

## Challenges

- AI-generated requirements still require human review.
- Sensitive project datavraises privacy concerns.
- AI may misunderstand business context.
- Outputs depend on the quality of prompts and input data.

## Summary

AI is changing how software requirements are gathered, analyzed, documented, and managed. Although AI significantly improves productivity and accuracy, human expertise remains essential for validating requirements and making final business decisions. As AI tools continue to evolve, they will become valuable assistants rather than replacements for requirement engineers.
